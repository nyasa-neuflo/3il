Hidden Gems:
5 Waterfalls You Won’t Find on the Main Tourist Map
27 December 2025
Kerala's famous falls like Athirappilly and Palaruvi get all the love, but the real treasures hide deeper in forests,villages, and hillsides where locals keep quiet. These five offbeat waterfalls reward the curious with pristine pools, zero crowds, and that "I found asecret" thrill.
1. Keezharkuthu Falls – The Rainbow Cascade (Idukki)
Near Thodupuzha, this 200-foot "RainbowWaterfalls" plunges through rubber and pineapple plantations. Sunlighthits just right to create actual rainbows in the mist. Short trek through spicetrails leads to natural pools perfect for dips. Rock climbing and camping spotsnearby make it an adventure hub. Locals call it their hidden swimming hole—wayless crowded than nearby tourist spots.​
2. Kumbhavurutty Falls – Forest Pool Paradise (Pathanamthitta)
Deep in Konni forest near Shendurney WildlifeSanctuary, Kumbhavurutty's multi-level cascades form inviting pools amid thickgreenery. Easy access from the road but feels worlds away. Perfect for picnicsor quiet swims. Monsoon swells it dramatically; dry season reveals rockyslides. Wildlife spotting bonus—elephants roam nearby. Bordering Tamil Nadu, itstays under the radar.​
3. Thoovanam Falls – Chinnar Sanctuary Secret (Idukki)
Tucked inside Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary nearMarayoor, Thoovanam ("mist drops") requires a guided trek throughthorny scrub and grasslands. The reward? A secluded 40-meter fall into emeraldpools surrounded by wild elephants' territory. Grizzled giant squirrels andrare butterflies make it a nature jackpot. Permit needed, but worth thepaperwork.​
4. Nayakkad Falls – Wayanad's Quiet Beauty (Wayanad)
18km from Kalpetta, Nayakkad flows gently intoserene pools perfect for reflection (or reflection photos). Short jungle walkthrough bamboo groves. Less dramatic than Meenmutty but infinitely morepeaceful—no vendors, no crowds. Best October-May when water's steady but notraging. Local favorite for family dips.​
5. Kuthumkal Waterfall – Munnar's Cliff Drop (Idukki)
15km from Munnar town, Kuthumkal crashes downrocky cliffs surrounded by coffee plantations and dense forest. Monsoon turnsit into a roaring beast; post-rain reveals tiered pools. Moderate trek rewardedwith panoramic hill views. Feels like Munnar's private backyard—tea bushesframe the scene perfectly.​
Why these stay hidden
Short treks (30min-2hrs), no direct bus access, and zero commercialization keep them secret. Go with locals or guides for safety and stories. Monsoon (June-Sep) maximizes flow but tests footing; winter offers calmer swims. Pack water, snacks, leech socks, and camera—social media silence helps preserve the magic.Skip the Instagram-famous falls. Chase these five instead. Kerala's best secrets belong to those willing to walk a little further.
